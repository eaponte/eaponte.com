About

I have been involved with technology, software, and hardware most of my life. I started my career in IT as a computer technician, having built and repaired hundreds of computers over many years.

I then migrated into technical support, working in a variety of industries including systems and networking sales, computer and electronics manufacturing, and healthcare. Working in healthcare IT, I worked in hospitals, cancer centers, and home healthcare industries. Working in these industries allowed me to move into software support and QA.

After years of working in software support and QA, I decided that I wanted to progress into writing code and learned HTML and CSS on my own, and later taking front-end development coursework at the New York Code and Design Academy in the financial district.

I now actively work in web development, building sites for a variety of clients using HTML, CSS, Javascript, jQuery, WordPress, ASP.NET and other technologies and tools.

Outside of the IT field, I have had a life-long interest in martial arts with a background that includes Okinawan karate, Muay Thai, Filipino stick fighting, and Russian martial art. To that end, I have also written countless articles for such publications as Black Belt magazine and World of Martial Arts magazine.

I also love to travel, and have been to many places across three continents to include countries such as Taiwan, China, Japan, South Korea, Spain, Italy and others. My goal is to set foot on all seven continents.
